Account pairs

One Mastodon account, one Bluesky account, one active identity pair.

Trombalsky is organized around pairs of accounts. A pair means one Mastodon account plus one Bluesky account. You can create multiple pairs and switch between them quickly from the two-profile-image icon.

  • Use a complete Mastodon and Bluesky pair for a unified social workflow.
  • Create several pairs for personal, project, company, or community identities.
  • Use Trombalsky with only Mastodon or only Bluesky when you do not need both networks.
  • Switch the active pair instead of signing out and back in between identities.

Core views

Three network modes across the main workflows.

Main Feed, Notifications, Messages, Liked Posts, Bookmarks, and Search can be used in Mastodon, Bluesky, or Both mode. Profiles let you choose between the Mastodon and Bluesky profile for the active pair.

Network modes

Choose the network scope that fits the moment.

Mastodon

  • Fediverse timelines and accounts.
  • Search, hashtags, trends, and server-aware behavior.
  • Push notifications for Mastodon activity.

Bluesky

  • AtProto posts, profiles, and conversations.
  • Bluesky notifications and messages.
  • Push notifications for Bluesky activity.

Both

  • Combined views for feeds, notifications, messages, likes, bookmarks, and search.
  • Fast movement between paired identities.
  • Clear network origin for mixed content.

About

A side project by Krystian Kozerawski.

Trombalsky is written in SwiftUI by Krystian Kozerawski, a developer at Apparent Software, also known for Eter, a radio app for iOS, macOS, watchOS, tvOS, and Android.

The name comes from Tomasz Trąbalski, the fictional elephant from Julian Tuwim's Polish poem "Słoń Trąbalski." The spelling became Trombalsky for easier pronunciation outside Poland, echoing how Polish names were sometimes adapted by replacing diacritics and changing "ski" to "sky." It fits an app that brings together Mastodon, named after an extinct relative of elephants, and Bluesky.

Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 23, 2026

Trombalsky is a native app for Mastodon and Bluesky. It is built to avoid advertising, third-party tracking, and unnecessary data collection. The app connects to the Mastodon instances and Bluesky/AtProto services that you choose, and it uses a Trombalsky push relay only to deliver notifications.

This policy explains what information is processed by the app, by the Trombalsky push relay, and by third-party services needed for the app to work.

Data We Do Not Collect

  • We do not use advertising SDKs.
  • We do not use third-party tracking SDKs.
  • We do not sell personal data.
  • We do not use your data to track you across apps or websites.
  • We do not collect location data.
  • We do not collect contacts.
  • We do not collect analytics unless a future version states otherwise in this policy.

Account Sign-In And Credentials

Trombalsky lets you sign in to Mastodon and Bluesky accounts.

  • Mastodon sign-in uses the Mastodon instance selected by you.
  • Bluesky sign-in uses Bluesky/AtProto services.
  • Access tokens and account credentials used by the app are stored locally on your device, such as in the system Keychain where appropriate.
  • Trombalsky does not use these credentials for advertising, analytics, or tracking.

Your Mastodon instance and Bluesky/AtProto services have their own privacy policies and terms. Content and account data sent to those services is governed by those services.

User Content

Trombalsky processes user content only as needed for app functionality. This may include:

  • posts, replies, reposts, likes, bookmarks, and other social actions;
  • direct messages or conversations where supported by the connected service;
  • profile text, avatars, banners, list images, and other account metadata you choose to edit;
  • photos, videos, or files you select for upload;
  • search queries sent to Mastodon or Bluesky services;
  • URLs used for opening links or generating link previews.

When you post, upload, send, search, or interact with content, that information is sent to the Mastodon or Bluesky service needed to perform the action.

Local App Data

Trombalsky stores some information locally on your device to make the app work, such as:

  • app preferences;
  • account and account-pair settings;
  • cached posts, conversations, media metadata, and timeline state;
  • notification preferences;
  • draft or compose-related state where supported.

Local data remains on your device unless it is sent to Mastodon, Bluesky, Apple services, or the Trombalsky push relay as described in this policy.

Push Notifications

If you enable notifications, Trombalsky uses Apple Push Notification service and a Trombalsky relay at push.trombalsky.app.

The relay is used only for notification delivery. It is not used for advertising, analytics, or tracking.

The relay may process information required to route notifications, such as:

  • APNs device tokens;
  • the push environment, such as sandbox or production;
  • Mastodon account or subscription information needed to forward Mastodon Web Push notifications;
  • Bluesky DIDs and notification-matching information needed to deliver Bluesky notifications;
  • notification payload data needed to send the notification to your device.

For Mastodon notifications, Mastodon Web Push payloads are encrypted by the Mastodon server and decrypted on your device by the app's notification extension where supported. The relay forwards the payload to Apple Push Notification service.

For Bluesky notifications, the relay watches public Bluesky/AtProto event streams, matches events relevant to registered users, and generates notification text required for APNs delivery.

You can disable notifications in the app or in system settings. Disabling notifications may remove or stop active push registrations where supported.

App Store Purchases

Trombalsky uses Apple's StoreKit for subscriptions and lifetime purchases. Purchase processing, billing, refunds, and subscription management are handled by Apple.

Trombalsky checks StoreKit entitlements to determine whether the app is unlocked. Trombalsky does not receive your full payment card details.

Permissions

Trombalsky may request system permissions only when needed for app functionality:

  • Notifications: to alert you about Mastodon and Bluesky activity.
  • Photos and media picker: to let you attach media to posts or profile/list updates.
  • Files/importer on supported platforms: to let you choose files or media to upload.
  • Pasteboard: when you explicitly paste content into compose fields or copy content from the app.

Third-Party Services

Trombalsky depends on these services for core functionality:

  • Mastodon instances selected by the user.
  • Bluesky and AtProto services.
  • Apple Push Notification service.
  • Apple StoreKit and App Store services for purchases.
  • Trombalsky push relay at push.trombalsky.app.
  • External websites opened by user action.

These services may process information under their own privacy policies.

Data Retention

Data stored locally in the app remains on your device until you delete it through the app, remove accounts, clear app data, or uninstall the app.

Push relay registration data is retained only as needed to deliver notifications and manage active registrations. Server logs, if any, should be limited to operational and security needs.

Mastodon, Bluesky, Apple, and external websites may retain data according to their own policies.

Children

Trombalsky is not directed to children. Users must meet the age requirements of the App Store and of the Mastodon or Bluesky services they use.

Changes To This Policy

If Trombalsky changes how it handles data, this policy will be updated. Important changes may also be announced in the app or on the Trombalsky website.
